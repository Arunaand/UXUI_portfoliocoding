Portfolio Case Study: From Concept to Code

Overview

Aruna Andrew is an accomplished UX designer from the University of Texas Austin's UX/UI program. She has led her team towards successfully delivering websites, landing pages, logos, email campaigns, and social media content. Her strengths lie in developing design strategies based on user and business requirements, specializing in user research, usability testing, information architecture, and interaction design. Aruna creates interfaces that delight users and foster brand loyalty.


My Role:

User Research, Sketching, Wireframing, Prototyping, Coding.

Tools I used:

Figma, HTML,CSS,Wix,Github,Google Drive, Bootstrap, JavaScript.

Problem

Over the course of 24 weeks, our team was given a diverse set of case studies and projects to work on. These tasks required us to employ a wide range of skills, including analysis, research, ideation, prototyping, and testing of various design concepts. We put a lot of effort into ensuring we thoroughly understood each project's requirements and objectives before starting work. We then brainstormed ideas, created rough drafts, and tested our designs until we were confident that they met all of the criteria and goals set out for us. This process ensured that we delivered high-quality and well-considered final products.



Solution

As part of our project, we went through several stages to ensure the end product met our requirements and expectations. Firstly, we began by sketching wireframes to create a visual representation of the layout and functionality of the product. Next, we added UI content to the wireframes to determine how the user interface would look and feel. We then iterated the designs, making changes and adjustments as necessary to improve the overall user experience. Finally, we retested the prototypes to ensure that they were functioning as intended and that the end product met our standards for quality and usability.


Opportunity

Through a thorough analysis of our design process, we identified critical insights that enabled us to make essential improvements and adjustments. By delving into the data, we could pinpoint specific areas that required attention and make data-driven decisions that led to more effective and efficient designs. This approach not only allowed us to identify and address issues early on but also helped us create a better end product that met the needs of our users.

My Experience

I did not have background in coding, however, I went onto every possible website, YouTube tutorial, and finally bought a HTML book to learn the language.

Was it stressful?

You bet it was!

Did I enjoy it?

Absolutely!

After sitting down infront of my desktop, trying, failing, figuring it out, and sometimes walking away from it, I finally am proud to say, I was able to code my Portfolio!
